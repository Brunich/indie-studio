World Overview

Emberveil is a single continent traversed west-to-east, then south toward The Scar. The eight gym towns are laid out across five distinct regions, each with its own culture, creature palette, and flavor of the Unsheathed threat running underneath the gym challenge. The gym badges are called Veil Seals — wax sigils pressed with the gym leader's creature's emblem.

Leader — Maren the Hearthkeeper

Maren is in her 50s, grandmother-age, beloved by the entire village. She has tended Ashgate's communal flame — lit from the original Scarring embers — for thirty years. She does not fight to win; she fights to teach. Her creatures are well-loved and veteran, and she speaks to them by name mid-battle.

Battle style: Status effects + Fire Spin to lock the opponent, then methodical pressure. She has an Embralcinder that has been with her since it was an Embral egg.

Gym Puzzle

A sequence of ceremonial hearth-chambers. The player must light the correct braziers in the order dictated by a poem carved into the entrance arch (Ashenveil oral tradition). Get it wrong and a NPC sends you back to try again — friendly, not punishing.

"The Walk begins at fire. Everything you'll learn out there — every friend, every fight, every choice — was already promised in the flame you left behind."— Maren, upon defeating her
REWARD: Cinder Seal + TM Ember Whirl

Leader — Captain Vesper

A weathered woman in her 40s, retired naval commander, now runs the port's official creature inspection service. Cold professional demeanor. Her battle philosophy is the Endless Current: apply constant pressure from every angle until the opponent has no more moves.

Battle style: Rain Dance + Water-type offense, Frothound lead, switches frequently to maintain weather advantage.

Gym Puzzle

The gym is built inside a dry-docked ship. Players must redirect water flow through a series of valves and hatches to drain or flood chambers, opening doors. Navigation puzzles — Tidebreakers love clever routing.

Narrative Hook — THE UNSHEATHED ARRIVE

◈ FIRST UNSHEATHED ENCOUNTER

Before the gym battle, a group of Unsheathed agents attempt to extort the port's trading licenses — leveraging their creature force against Vesper's docked fleet. Rex Vale is here mediating a contract dispute and intervenes. This is Riven and Rex's first proper meeting. Vesper grudgingly gives Rex credit, but warns Riven: "He works for whoever pays. Remember that."

REWARD: Tidal Seal + TM Aqua Current

Leader — Elder Venna

Ancient. No one knows how old. Speaks slowly, and every sentence lands. She has a Folivian that is large enough to be ridden, bonded for over 40 years. She can communicate with it in a gesture-language that looks like dance. She finds Pokémon-style "winning" battles mildly amusing but will take you seriously if you earn it.

Battle style: Sleep Powder → Leech Seed → stall. Forces long attrition battles. Very irritating to fight. Very instructive.

Gym Puzzle

A web-maze in the canopy. Silk threads mark pathways — white threads are safe, colored threads are traps that spring Spidrel encounters. Players who stop to examine threads closely (interact button) can read the pattern. Rushing gets you trapped repeatedly.

Narrative Hook — PYRA IS INTRODUCED HERE

Pyra's resonance device has amplified a Folimp population in the Academy's restricted wing — they've nested in the scroll library. The player helps solve this using battle and puzzle. Pyra joins after the gym badge is earned.

REWARD: Web Seal + TM Thorn Bind

Leader — Harrok, Clan Ironpeak Champion

Loud, proud, magnetic. Won the highland championship four years running. He challenges every trainer who enters his city to an informal street battle before letting them register for the gym — the Highlands way of determining if you're worth taking seriously. He has Aldric's old rival's team, essentially.

Battle style: Aggressive, direct, no tricks. Fast Electric attackers, Voltuxen as ace. If you try status games on him he just attacks faster. Hates stalling, will call you out on it.

Gym Puzzle

A tournament bracket. The player fights their way through 5 trainers in a single-elimination format, without healing between matches (healing items allowed). Tests party depth. The other trainers taunt and comment on your style during matches.

Narrative Hook — ALDRIC

◈ ALDRIC JOINS / TOURNAMENT FRAUD EXPOSED

Aldric is in the city mediating a dispute. Riven, with Rex's data, exposes that the current sponsorship consortium funding the tournament draws from Unsheathed accounts. Harrok is publicly confronted. He had no idea. He strips the corrupt organizers' access. Aldric, finally seeing the same corruption he was exiled for being publicly named, agrees to travel south.

REWARD: Storm Seal + TM Thunder Clap

◈ The Necromancer City — Cultural Concept

Duskwall Necropolis is built directly into and over an ancient burial plateau — a city where the living and the dead share the same streets. Every building is half-tomb, half-home. Residents wear white (death color in their culture, not mourning but honoring), and Ghost-type creatures wander freely, unafraid of humans. At night the city glows faint violet from ambient Veilfire.

The Necromancers aren't evil — they're scholars of the transition. They believe death is simply the moment a person's Veilfire becomes Remembered Veilfire, and that it can still communicate if you learn to listen. The Seer class trains for decades to have conversations with the dead through Ghost-type intermediaries.

Visual references: Think Lavender Town meets a Mesoamerican Day of the Dead aesthetic. Skull motifs everywhere but warm, not horror. Candles. Bone-white towers. Purple sky from Veilfire concentration. The Necropolis sits at the edge of The Scar's northern tip — the ambient energy is thicker here.

Leader — Malachar the Seer

Gender-ambiguous, ageless-seeming, speaks in measured statements that feel like prophecy. Partners with a Bonehound and a Necroveil. Has been the gym leader for 22 years and the city's chief Seer for 35. Nyx holds Malachar in the deepest respect — they trained under the same tradition.

Battle style: Will-o-Wisp burns, Hex combo, Phantom Force for positioning, Bonehound's tracking prevents escape. Battles with Malachar feel like being hunted — they know where you'll move before you do.

Gym Puzzle

The gym is a ritual labyrinth. Players receive a white candle from an elder at the entrance — Ghost-type creatures will let you pass as long as your candle is lit. Wind gusts (caused by interacting with wrong objects) can blow it out, resetting your progress to the last lit lantern checkpoint. The puzzle is about moving carefully, reading the environment, never rushing.

Narrative Hook — THE GUILD MASSACRE REVEALED

◈ NYX'S HISTORY / WHAT'S UNDER THE SCAR

Malachar tells Riven the truth the Council has suppressed: the Veil Walker guild was not destroyed by a Scar surge. It was deliberately silenced because they were the only ones who could hear the entity at the Scar's base — and what it was saying was "they're coming." The Guild's final transmission, preserved in a Bonehound's Veilfire memory, is: "It is not a threat. It is trying to warn us. The Unsheathed know what it's protecting."

"We do not fear what lies beneath the Scar. We fear what wants to open it."— Malachar the Seer, Duskwall Necropolis
REWARD: Bone Seal + TM Shadow Hold

Leader — Vera Frost, Chief Architect

Crystalspire's Architect class builds with ice and organic steel-crystal grown from glacier deposits. Vera is the foremost practitioner — the city's impossible geometric towers are her work. She approaches battle like engineering: every weakness can be measured, every outcome calculated. She's not cold-hearted, just very precise.

Battle style: Hail weather + Blizzard sweepers, Glacinth's Defense Curl + Ice Wall combo. She plays to remove all of your options before attacking.

Gym Puzzle

Sliding ice floor puzzle with crystal prisms that redirect light beams. Players must position prisms to light all four corner pylons simultaneously to unseal the leader's chamber. Classic mechanic, beautifully presented.

Narrative Hook

Pyra realizes her resonance device, which reacts to Veilfire, is registering a signal here that shouldn't exist — an Unsheathed research station embedded in the glacier, drilling toward the Scar's northern terminus. The team destroys it. The Unsheathed begin actively hunting them.

REWARD: Crystal Seal + TM Glacier Wall

SCARMOUTH

Leader — Dread, Elder Scar Warden

Former Council Warden who refused to follow the suppression orders. Lives in self-imposed exile at the Scar's edge. Massive, quiet, ancient-feeling though not actually old — the proximity to Veilfire ages people differently. His Scarfang is the largest Dragon-type creature Riven has ever seen. He respects only those who've proven they can stand at the Scar's edge without flinching.

Battle style: Dragon Dance to buff speed and attack, then Dragon Rush sweep. Single-minded but overwhelming.

Gym Puzzle

Riven must prove themselves to Dread's apprentice Wardens in three escalating trials: a creature knowledge quiz (lore-based, tests attention to Pokédex entries), a solo survival battle against wild Scarfang (held area), and a navigation test through the Scar perimeter's unstable terrain.

Narrative Hook — MARA'S RESEARCH STATION

◈ MARA'S LAST CAMP

Dread was the last person to speak with Mara Solís. He shows Riven her sealed research camp, preserved exactly as she left it. The final journal entry: "I've made contact. Not sound, not light — Veilfire resonance, like a creature offering a bond. It's terrified. It knows what the Unsheathed want to do. I need to find a way to tell it we're not all like them."

REWARD: Scar Seal + TM Dragon Charge
◈ GYM 08 — VEILWATCH COUNCIL CAPITAL
VEILWATCH SPIRE
The High Warden's Chamber — Final Gym
TYPEPSYCHIC FIRE
LEADERHigh Warden Cassiel
SEAL">Veil Seal

Leader — High Warden Cassiel

Head of the Veilwatch Council. Impeccably dressed. Perfectly reasonable in every conversation. Champions progressive creature welfare reforms publicly. Has been secretly routing Warden intelligence to the Unsheathed for fifteen years to maintain "stability." Believes — genuinely — that controlled extraction from the Scar is better than the alternative of letting the underground entity eventually breach the surface on its own terms.

This makes Cassiel a genuinely interesting villain: they're not wrong that the entity poses some risk. They're wrong about the method and catastrophically wrong about what the entity actually wants.

Battle style: Future Sight prediction + Fire-type cleanup. Cassiel plays three turns ahead. Refuses to acknowledge any strategy that isn't calculated.

Gym Puzzle

A Hall of Records — the player must use a council archive terminal to locate the correct clearance level to reach Cassiel. The "puzzle" involves reading Council documents that incrementally reveal Cassiel's complicity. By the time you reach the gym battle, you already know. The battle starts with Riven saying: "You knew about my mother."

Narrative Climax

◈ THE REVELATION

Cassiel admits everything after the battle. Offers Riven a deal: walk away, Riven gets full Warden credentials, their mother's research is formally credited and published, and the Unsheathed are "managed." Refuse and Cassiel will use Council authority to revoke every badge and block access to the Deep Spire. The player has no good choice — except choosing to go anyway.

REWARD: Veil Seal + HM Veil Break (all eight seals needed for Deep Spire)

The Parallel Story — The Unsheathed Arc

While the gym journey gives structure, the main story runs alongside it. Every 1-2 gyms, a story beat. The Unsheathed move from background menace to direct antagonists. Riven moves from personal mission to world-stakes realization.

CHAPTER 0 — BEFORE THE WALK · Cinder Village

Riven collects the Embral left by Mara. Nana Cais seals the letter. Warden Tolá reveals Mara left a report the Council received but never acted on. The ember of the mystery is lit.

CHAPTER 1 — THE WATCHERS · Route 1 → Gym 2

Nyx joins on Route 1 — she's been tracking Unsheathed movements toward the coast. First Unsheathed confrontation at Tidelock Port. Rex Vale appears. The team doesn't trust each other yet. The Unsheathed agents destroy a Warden station on their way out — a warning.

CHAPTER 2 — THE DEVICE · Gym 3 → Gym 4

Pyra joins after the Canopy gym. She doesn't know the group's background yet — she's excited about the Embral and wants to run readings on Sable's Veilfire output. Rex reveals to Riven (privately) that he has Mara's tablets. Pyra starts to decode them. The Unsheathed break into the Loreweave Academy overnight — they want Pyra's research.

CHAPTER 3 — THE TOURNAMENT & THE EXILE · Gym 4

The Voltcrest tournament fraud is exposed. Aldric joins. The team is now complete. The Unsheathed send a direct message: give us the tablets or we will ensure each of you cannot complete the Walk. The team votes to continue south. First battle against an Unsheathed commander — a real fight.

CHAPTER 4 — THE NECROPOLIS TRUTH · Gym 5 ★ MAJOR REVELATION

Malachar reveals the Guild massacre truth. Nyx hears a preserved memory from her old mentor through a Spectryn. The entity under the Scar tried to warn the Veil Walkers — the Unsheathed silenced them because they knew the entity wasn't a monster, they knew it was a guardian, and they needed everyone to believe otherwise to justify opening the Scar.

Pyra completes her device. It can now read Veilfire at depth — and it's registering something massive beneath the badlands. The entity is not at the Scar. The entity IS the Scar's source. It seals the rift from below. Opening the Scar permanently would kill it.

CHAPTER 5 — THE HUNT · Routes 6-7

The Unsheathed now move openly. A full squad ambushes the team on the glacier road, separating Aldric. Rex is captured and briefly used as leverage (Aldric rescues him — which is what breaks Rex's neutral contract stance). Pyra's device is nearly destroyed. Riven reaches Dread at the Scar perimeter alone except for their creatures and Nyx.

CHAPTER 6 — MARA'S CAMP · Gym 7

At Dread's camp, Riven reads Mara's final journal. Her last entry was written the night she disappeared — she was going into the Scar to make contact, not scouting its perimeter. She's not dead. She's been communicating with the entity, and she's using its Veilfire to preserve herself at the rift's edge. The entity has been waiting for Riven specifically — Sable carries Mara's emotional signature. The creature has been delivering a message for fourteen years.

CHAPTER 7 — THE SPIRE · Gym 8

The confrontation with Cassiel. The Hall of Records reveals everything. The gym battle carries real stakes. Cassiel's offer afterward is genuinely tempting — Riven has to want to refuse it, not just know they should. The game asks the player to sit with the bad choice for a moment before continuing.

The Unsheathed — Antagonist Structure

The Unsheathed are not a criminal gang. They are a ideological faction — true believers who have convinced themselves that controlled extraction of the entity's Veilfire would democratize elemental power across Emberveil and end the Council's monopoly on creature-related resources. Some of their arguments are even correct. They are wrong in the worst way: completely right about the problem, completely wrong about the solution.

◈ UNSHEATHED HIERARCHY
COMMAND STRUCTURE

The Director (unseen until Act 3)

Identity unknown until Mara's tablets are fully decoded. The Director founded the Unsheathed from inside the Council — they are the architect of the suppression policy and the extraction plan simultaneously. Wants to be the one who "solves" the Scar problem, on their terms.

High Warden Cassiel (Gym 8 Leader)

The Unsheathed's political arm. Runs interference on Warden investigations, routes intelligence to field teams, ensures the Council's public face remains clean while operations continue below.

Commander Voss (recurring field boss)

Not the same Voss as Nyx's creature — intentional irony. The field commander. First appears at Tidelock, escalates as the player approaches the Scar. Respects strength. After the third defeat, there's a scene where they admit to Riven that they've seen the entity's Veilfire resonance and it doesn't match their briefing. Their faith in the Director cracks.

Rank and file

Young, idealistic, deliberately recruited from people the Council has failed — exiled Highland trainers, Canopy researchers whose grants were denied, Tidebreak traders ruined by Council trade restrictions. The Unsheathed are correct that the Council is corrupt. They are not the answer.

The Deep Spire — Elite Four & Champion

The Deep Spire is located at the Scar's southern terminus — the point closest to the entity. It requires all eight Veil Seals and the Veil Break HM to access. It looks like a natural formation from the outside: a spiral of ancient black rock erupting from the Scar's edge, warm to the touch, faintly luminous at night.

◈ ELITE 1
VESPER (REMATCH)
Water / Ice — "The Current Never Ends"

Captain Vesper has resigned from her gym post and chosen to serve the Deep Spire as gatekeeper. "If you couldn't beat me there, you can't beat what's ahead." Her team is evolved and optimized. She respects Riven now.

◈ ELITE 2
MALACHAR (REMATCH)
Ghost / Dark — "What You Remember Is What You Are"

Malachar traveled to the Deep Spire after the Necropolis revelation. "The entity told me it has been sending memories upward through Ghost-type Veilfire for centuries. We just never knew how to read them." Their team includes a Veildark that has never appeared in the wild above ground before.

◈ ELITE 3
DREAD (REMATCH)
Dragon / Rock — "The Scar Made Something That Couldn't Hurt You"

Dread is here not as a challenge but as a test of intention. He fights differently from his gym battle — more emotional, as if the proximity to the entity is affecting his Scarfang.

◈ ELITE 4
HARROK (UNEXPECTED)
Electric / Fighting — "Proving It Was Never About Me"

Harrok arrived after Riven exposed the tournament fraud. He's here because "I need to prove to myself I can fight without a corrupt system propping me up." He's the emotional gut-check — the hardest non-champion battle because he has nothing to lose and everything to prove.

◈ CHAMPION — THE DEEP SPIRE SUMMIT
DR. MARA SOLÍS — "I Promised I'd Wait"
All types — the researcher who stayed

Mara is alive. She has been here for fourteen years, in voluntary communion with the entity, preserved by its Veilfire while she learned to communicate with it. She has the deepest bond with Veilborn creatures ever recorded — her party of six includes creatures from every region, bonded by the entity's awareness flowing through her.

The battle between Riven and Mara is not aggressive. It is the last test the entity requires before it will accept humans as allies rather than threats — proof that a human trained in full knowledge of what Veilfire is and what the entity is worth can face its full expression and choose care over dominance.

"I wrote you that letter every year. I couldn't send it. I thought you'd come find me when the Walk started. I knew you would. Sable knows your footsteps."— Mara Solís, Champion of the Deep Spire

After the battle: Mara and Riven stand at the Scar's edge. The Veilfire below rises gently — the entity's version of warmth, of acknowledgment. The seal on the Scar holds. The Unsheathed's final attack is repelled by the combined Veilfire of every bonded creature in Riven's party, all four companions' creatures, and the entity itself. Some things cannot be weaponized. Some things can only be witnessed.
